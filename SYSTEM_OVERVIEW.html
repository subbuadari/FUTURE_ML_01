⚙️ Feature Engineering Details

Time-Based Features

The system extracts temporal patterns from dates:

  • Day of month (1-31)
  • Month (1-12)
  • Quarter (1-4)
  • Day of week (Monday-Sunday)
  • Is weekend (binary indicator)
  • Day of year (1-365)

Lag Features

Previous sales values capture momentum and patterns:

  • Previous day's sales (1-day lag)
  • Previous week's sales (7-day lag)
  • Previous month's sales (30-day lag)

Rolling Averages

Moving averages smooth out noise and reveal trends:

  • 7-day moving average (short-term trend)
  • 30-day moving average (long-term trend)

Why Gradient Boosting?

Gradient Boosting achieved the highest R² score (≈0.88), explaining about 88% of the variance in sales. This means predictions are highly aligned with actual sales, making it reliable for business decisions. The model's Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of approximately $200-300 provides a reasonable confidence interval for inventory and staffing planning.

📊 Model Evaluation Metrics Explained

R² Score (Coefficient of Determination)

Shows what percentage of sales variation the model explains.

  • 0.85+ : Excellent - Very reliable for decisions
  • 0.70-0.85: Good - Can be trusted for planning
  • 0.50-0.70: Fair - Use with domain expertise
  • <0.50: Poor - Needs improvement

RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)

Average prediction error in dollars. Penalizes large errors more heavily.

  • Useful for identifying outliers
  • Helps set error bounds
  • Lower values indicate better fit
  • Scale: Same as target variable

MAE (Mean Absolute Error)

Average absolute prediction error - most intuitive metric.

  • Easy to interpret: "Off by $X on average"
  • Not overly sensitive to outliers
  • Most useful for business planning
  • Similar scale to RMSE but less extreme

💡 Business Recommendations

📦 Inventory Planning

Use the 30-day forecast to prepare stock levels. Order more goods for forecasted peak sales days and reduce inventory during predicted low-demand periods to avoid overstocking and storage costs.

👥 Staffing Decisions

Schedule extra staff during high-sales forecast days when customer volume will be higher. Plan maintenance, training, or reduced hours during low-sales periods to optimize labor costs.

💰 Cash Flow Management

Budget expected daily revenue based on the forecast. Plan for lower cash inflows during predicted dips and prepare for higher capital needs during peak periods.

📢 Marketing & Promotions

Time promotional campaigns for periods with lower forecasted demand to boost sales. Capitalize on natural peak periods by managing inventory and staffing appropriately.

📈 Continuous Monitoring

Track actual sales against forecasts weekly. When deviations occur, investigate causes and update the model monthly with new data for continuous improvement and accuracy.

⚠️ Understanding Limitations

Assumptions Made

  • Historical patterns continue into the future
  • No major market disruptions or external shocks
  • Regular seasonality repeats annually
  • Relationships between features and sales remain stable
  • No significant competitor actions or market changes

When to Adjust Forecasts Manually

  • During major promotional campaigns
  • Around new product launches
  • During holiday seasons with unusual demand
  • After changes in store operations or layout
  • Following significant competitor actions

Continuous Improvement

The model's accuracy improves with:

  • More data (model improves with 2+ years of history)
  • Regular retraining (monthly updates essential)
  • Feature enhancements (adding marketing spend, weather, etc.)
  • External data integration (holidays, competitor data)
